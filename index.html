Langton's Ant

Langton's Ant is a two-dimentional turing machine, invented by Chris Langton in 1986. An ant starts in the middle of a grid and each "step" switches it's current square's color, and moves to a new square based on two rules:

  • at a light square, turn 90° right and move forward 1 square
  • at a dark square, turn 90° left and move forward 1 square

This visualization allows for multiple ants at a time, and includes a multi-color option, where a square's color value alternates with each color change. More information at: Langton's Ant

To add a new ant, simply click anywhere on the gird, or click on the random ant button. If there are no ants on the grid when starting, an ant will automatically be placed in the center.
